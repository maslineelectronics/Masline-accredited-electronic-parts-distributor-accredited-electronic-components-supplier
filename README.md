## Quality Management Systems

Accredited distributors and suppliers rely on formal Quality Management Systems (QMS) to standardize processes and maintain consistency across global operations. These systems ensure that every order, regardless of size or destination, meets predefined quality and compliance benchmarks.

### Common Certifications:
- **ISO 9001:** Quality management for manufacturing and distribution.  
- **AS9120:** Aerospace and defense distribution standards.  
- **ISO 14001:** Environmental management certification.  
- **RoHS and REACH:** Compliance for hazardous materials and chemical safety.  
- **ESD Standards:** Protection from electrostatic discharge during handling.  

Through accreditation and certification, every step of the sourcing process is documented, measured, and continuously improved.

## Inspection and Verification Processes

Quality begins with inspection. Accredited distributors implement comprehensive testing and verification protocols to ensure the authenticity and functionality of every component before shipment.

### Typical Verification Steps:
1. **Visual Inspection:** Checking labeling, date codes, and packaging integrity.  
2. **Microscopic Examination:** Identifying surface inconsistencies or re-marking.  
3. **Electrical Testing:** Confirming component performance specifications.  
4. **X-ray Analysis:** Verifying internal die structure and bond wires.  
5. **Functional Testing:** Assessing actual in-circuit or design-level behavior.  

These verification steps guarantee that only approved and fully compliant parts are delivered to the customer.
